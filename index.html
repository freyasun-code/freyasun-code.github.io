Project 0: Becoming Friends with Your Camera

1. Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

When I take a selfie very close to my face, the wide-angle lens exaggerates perspective. My nose looks bigger, and my face seems squished and stretched out. This distortion makes my face look unnatural and less flattering. Taking a selfie from farther away makes the facial proportions look more natural and closer to how I actually look.

2. Architectural Perspective Compression

I kept the bus stop sign about the same size in both pictures. In the first picture, the building looks much closer to the sign and the whole picture looks flattened and compressed, compared to the second picture.

Zoom in

Walk down, no zoom

Guess: The camera uses a perspective projection matrix for spatial transformation, which makes the depth values nonlinear. This means that objects closer to the camera change in scale much more rapidly, while distant objects change very little. As a result, when we step back and zoom in, the background appears larger and the image looks compressed.

3. The Dolly Zoom

I move the camera back and zoom in, keeping the resulting image roughly the same size. Then I got the classic film effect--dolly zoom.
